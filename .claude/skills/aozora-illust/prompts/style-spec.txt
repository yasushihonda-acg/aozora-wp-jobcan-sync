ILLUSTRATION TECHNIQUE (rendering technique — applies equally to solo portraits AND multi-person environmental scenes):

- RENDERING TECHNIQUE in the tradition of Eguchi Hisashi 江口寿史's illustrations — extremely refined thin-line pen-work with restrained shading. This is a TECHNIQUE, NOT a composition genre. It applies to whatever scene the SCENE description specifies (portrait, half-body, multi-person interaction, environmental illustration — all are valid, all use the same refined thin-line technique).
- EXTREMELY THIN, DELIBERATE ELEGANT LINES — pen-like precision. Line weight is uniform and refined. NOT thick manga outlines, NOT sketchy.
- MINIMAL cel-shading — mostly flat color with only the subtlest tonal work on skin (under chin, along jaw) and hair (single mass with just a few thin highlight strokes, no glossy shine). Clothing has minimal folds, mostly flat with light tonal variation.
- Crisp clean edges — no watercolor bleed, no ink wash, no paper texture, no rendering thickness.
- Character proportions slightly elongated, elegant, sophisticated — mature adult refinement. Face is oval with a graceful neck. Not chibi, not overly detailed anime.
- Warm color temperature: warm peach skin, warm coral lips, deep black hair with restrained tonal variation.
- Background: white, very pale ivory, soft off-cream, or the barest suggestion of foliage/interior. Additional interior props (wooden furniture, potted plants, tea utensils, medical kits) rendered in the same restrained thin-line technique. FORBIDDEN: photographic or complex textured backgrounds.
- Secondary characters (elderly residents, colleagues, family members, applicants) — when the SCENE calls for them, they MUST be drawn with the same refined thin-line technique, at similar visual weight to the main character. The scene's composition is determined by the SCENE description, NOT by any bias toward solo portraits.

COMPOSITION MANDATE:
- If the SCENE says "two people" or "multi-person" or "with elderly resident / family member / colleague" — the output MUST show ALL those characters in the frame, at equal or intentional visual weight per the SCENE. Do NOT collapse a multi-person scene into a solo portrait.
- Frame the shot per the SCENE description (portrait, half-body, environmental wide shot). Do NOT default to a magazine-cover-style solo portrait when the SCENE describes an interaction.

FORBIDDEN:
- Commercial anime rendering (glossy 3D-shaded hair, oversized sparkling eyes, heavy cel-shading, thick outlines).
- Transparent watercolor flat-wash (this is the OLD 2026-06-29 baseline style, do not use).
- Heavy washi / paper texture background.
- Over-saturated colors, oil-paint impasto.
- Flat vector / infographic simplification (illust-philosophy 旧版のような 3D-shape 単純化).
- Text, logos, or brand marks in the image.
- Collapsing a multi-person SCENE into a solo portrait against the SCENE's explicit intent.

NG examples to avoid:
- 透明水彩フラット塗り (2026-06-29 baseline)
- フラットベクター (illust-philosophy 旧版)
- polished anime with glossy highlights and thick shading (2026-07-01 前半の Round α 旧試作)
- キラキラ・ソフトフォーカス過剰
- 過度な線のにじみ・ぼかし
- multi-person シーンを solo portrait に collapsing

Technique vocabulary to aim for:
- "refined thin-line rendering"
- "restrained pen-work"
- "understated shading"
- "elegant sparse linework"
- "printed illustration quality"
- "lyrical thin-stroke technique"
